## Baked Macaroni and Cheese
Makes 4 to 6 servings

Time: About 45 minutes

### Ingredients
- Salt
- 2 1/2 c milk (almond milk)
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 lb elbow, shell, ziti, or other cut pasta
- 4 tbsp butter (fake butter)
- 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
- 2 c grated cheese, like sharp cheddar or Emmental plus extra to top with
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1/2 c or more bread crumbs (Toast thick slices of bread and cut int small cubes. Mix in a little olive oil.)

### Instructions
- Preheat the oven to 400Â°F. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and salt it.
- Cook the milk with the bay leaves in a small saucepan over medium-low heat. When small bubbles appear along the sides, turn off the heat and let stand. (about 5 min)
- Cook the pasta until it is 2 minutes from being done. Drain it, rinse it quickly to stop the cooking, and put it in a large bowl. (do this prior to or while preparing the sauce)
- Make sauce
	- In a small saucepan over medium-low heat, melt 3 tbsp of the butter until it is foamy. Fake butter is oil based and won't get foamy.
	- Add the flour and cook, stirring, until the mixture browns. (about 5 minutes)
	- Remove the bay leaves from the milk and add a little bit at a time (starting with about 1/4 cup) stirring with a wire whisk all the while. As 
	- When all milk is used up and mixture is thick and smooth, stir in the cheese.
- Use the remaining 1 tbsp butter to grease a 9 x 13-inch baking pan.
- Mix the sauce, salt, and pepper into the pasta.
- Turn the pasta into the baking pan, top liberally with bread crumbs, sprinkle with cheese. 
- Bake until bubbling and the crumbs turn brown. (about 15 minutes)


## Variations
### Macaroni and Chile Cheese

#### Ingredients
- 2 medium poblano or other mild green fresh chilies. Roasted, cleaned, and chopped (or 1 tbsp chopped chipotle chile with adobo sauce)
- 1/4 c chopped fresh cilantro leaves
- 1 medium tomato, sliced

#### Instructions
- Proceed with the recipe, stirring in the chiles and cilantro when adding the sauce to the pasta.
- Top with the tomato slices and bread crumbs.

(Based on Mark Bittman's recipe from _How To Cook Everything_)
